## Prototyping
Three prototypes were developed throughout the implementation phase of the project. Third prototype was adopted and is still in use.
![All_prototypes](/Implemenatation/Prototypes/images/All_prototypes.jpg)

### Prototype I - Humble beginnings
Prototype 1 was made purely to get the feel for all the components and learn what's the best way to orient/connect them. Important discovery from this phase was the USB-C port would not allow for all the ports to be on a single plane so a USB-C extender was bought and used in future prototypes to allow for this quality of life feature.

![Prototype1_1](/Implemenatation/Prototypes/images/Prototype1_1.jpg)







### Prototype II - Off-Dimensions 3D print
Prototype 2 was a 3D model in Fusion360, and printed at the university's lab using the Stratasys PolyJet Objet 260 CONNEX 3 3D printer - courtesy of the university lab. Note only the most critical part was printed to test dimensions in order to save materials. This prototype as well as the next one both were made with thermal consideration in mind (this device is going to be running ideally indefinitely - hence it needs good air flow ) - floor offsets were installed and mesh fan cover was planned. Major observations from this prototyping phase were the dimension inaccuracy (Internet lied - calipers fixed).
