## Stakeholder 1

 **Name** Dev team / Manager team (us)

 **Status** Positive

 **Expectations, interests** <br>
 We have positive expectations that our project will succed. For profit and personal use, we have it in our interest so that it succeeds.

 **Possible conflicts/benefits** <br>
 Trivially, we benefit very much if it succeeds. With proper management we hope to avoid any internal conflicts among the team members.

 **Team response** <br>
 To ensure project success, we are implementing the Scrum methodology dividing the work in sprints.

## Stakeholder 2

 **Name** Google LLC - namely Google Cloud services

 **Status** Negative

 **Expectations, interests** <br>
 Their expectation is that people keep using their products for free at the expense of having their data processed and thus exposing their privacy a bit. Or to be more specific they expect people will keep using their products not caring that their data is being processed. It's in their interest if our project fails.

 **Possible conflicts/benefits** <br>
 The obvious conflict is that people would use only one or the other. Sure, some people could use both, but that's unlikely.

 **Team response** <br>
 We will work to make sure that our services fulfill the user needs at a satisfiable level for the users, while ensuring the privacy of their data.

## Stakeholder 3

 **Name** Government - regulations/laws regarding data privacy

 **Status** Neutral

 **Expectations, interests** <br>
 Their interests and expectations are the same. That we follow the laws regarding data privacy.

 **Possible conflicts/benefits** <br>
 No benefits to discuss here. Conflicts would arise if we were to ignore the laws or to be more specific go against those laws, which would not be in our best interest.

 **Team response** <br>
 For that reason we will hire an external organization to perform periodic audits, making sure that our implementation doesn't go against the laws regarding data privacy.

## Stakeholder 4

 **Name** Students <br>
 \- mainly computer science ones, but the services can be used by anyone who would benefit from our provided services.

 **Status** Positive

 **Expectations, interests** <br>
 Their expectation is that they can get the services we offer on the premise that their data is kept private. This is also in their interest, as each user wants to keep their data private if possible.

 **Possible conflicts/benefits** <br>
 The benefits are obvious. A possible conflict could arise if some students don't want to pay for the services and would rather use products of other organizations, not minding that their data is being used for training AI or other stuff.

 **Team response** <br>
 We believe that our services are offered at a profitable price, for both parties.  
 However, for those who seem to think that isn't the case we plan on implementing the following strategy
 - free basic tier funded by the student's university - with limitations on the services
 - €5 per month for premium tier - more cloud storage and hosting capabilities
 
 ## Stakeholder 5

 **Name** University

 **Status** Neutral

 **Expectations, interests** <br>
 They don't have much expectations regarding our project. What's in their best interest is to fulfill their student's needs and wants while expending the least amount of resources.

 **Possible conflicts/benefits** <br>
 A possible benefit could be if they decide to invest in our project, so that our service becomes free for their students.

 **Team response** <br>
 Pitch the idea to the university and sign a contract so that our services are offered to students for free. Depending on how much they want to pay, students could either get the premium or free tier.
